CHAPTER 17
The Storm Before the Silence

The predawn air bit at Nesta’s skin as she woke, her body a map of aches and bruises from the survival test. She didn’t groan as she sat up, didn’t wince as she stretched her stiff limbs. The hollow place inside her chest didn’t allow for such things.

She dressed in silence, the gray gown clinging to her frame, the weight of it familiar now. The Illyrian camp was still asleep, the warriors sprawled in their furs, their snores a low rumble through the stone huts. She braided her hair with practiced efficiency, the coronet tight against her skull. The mirror showed a face that was all sharp angles, her cheekbones more pronounced than they’d been a month ago. She looked like a stranger.

Cassian’s question from last night echoed in her mind, but she shoved it aside. She didn’t have the energy to dissect it, to pick apart why it had lodged itself in her ribs like a splinter. *What do you want?* As if she even knew anymore.

The training grounds were already stirring when she stepped outside. Devlon stood at the center, his arms crossed, his scarred face unreadable. The warriors gathered around him, their eyes gleaming with something that made Nesta’s stomach twist. Anticipation. Malice.

“Today,” Devlon announced, his voice cutting through the morning quiet, “you will face Bellius in hand-to-hand combat. No weapons. No mercy.”

The warriors murmured, their gazes flicking to Nesta. She kept her face blank, but her fingers curled into fists at her sides.

Bellius stepped forward, his smirk a slash of cruelty. He was built like a bull, all thick muscle and brute strength. His eyes burned with hatred, and Nesta knew, without a doubt, that he would try to break her.

She swallowed hard, her throat dry. The survival test had left her exhausted, her body screaming for rest. But there was no time for that. Not here.

Devlon’s gaze locked onto her. “You will fight until one of you yields or is unable to continue.”

Nesta’s breath hitched. She had never yielded in her life. And she wasn’t about to start now.

The warriors formed a circle around the training grounds, their murmurs a low hum of anticipation. Nesta stepped into the center, her boots sinking into the damp earth. Bellius rolled his shoulders, his smirk never wavering.

She could feel the weight of their stares, the hunger in their eyes. They wanted blood. They wanted her to fail.

Nesta lifted her chin, her gaze locking onto Bellius. She wouldn’t give them the satisfaction.

Devlon’s voice rang out, sharp and final. “Begin.”

---

The first strike came swift and brutal, Bellius’s fist aimed straight for her throat. Nesta twisted at the last moment, his knuckles grazing her jaw instead. The impact sent a jolt through her bones, but she didn’t falter—she pivoted, driving her elbow into his ribs with a force that made him grunt.

The warriors roared, their voices a wall of sound, a chorus of bloodlust and anticipation. Nesta didn’t let herself look at them. She couldn’t afford the distraction.

Bellius recovered faster than she expected, his boot lashing out in a vicious kick. She blocked it with her forearm, but the force of it still sent her stumbling back. His laughter was a dark, mocking thing.

“Is that all, little mouse?” he taunted, circling her like a predator. “I expected more from Rhysand’s pet.”

Nesta bared her teeth. “I’m not his pet.”

“No?” Bellius feinted left, then struck right, his fist connecting with her side. Pain flared, bright and hot, but she swallowed the cry that threatened to escape. “Then prove it.”

She lunged, her movements precise, controlled. She wasn’t as strong as him, not yet, but she was faster. She ducked beneath his next swing and drove her knee into his gut. He doubled over with a wheeze, and for a moment, the crowd’s cheers faltered.

Then his hand shot out, gripping her braid and yanking her head back. Nesta’s breath hitched as pain lanced through her scalp, but she didn’t give him the satisfaction of a reaction. Instead, she slammed her heel down onto his instep.

Bellius snarled, releasing her, and she twisted away, her chest heaving. The warriors were on their feet now, their voices rising in a frenzy. She could feel their eyes on her, hungry, waiting for her to break.

She wouldn’t.

Bellius wiped blood from his split lip, his smirk widening. “You fight like a noblewoman playing at war.”

Nesta didn’t answer. She didn’t need to.

He came at her again, his strikes faster, harder. She blocked, countered, but he was relentless. A punch to her ribs sent her sprawling, her breath knocked from her lungs. The pain was white-hot, searing, but she forced herself up, her legs trembling.

Bellius laughed. “Pathetic.”

The word ignited something in her. Something dark and furious. Nesta lunged, her fist connecting with his jaw in a solid, satisfying crack. His head snapped to the side, and for a heartbeat, the crowd fell silent.

Then Bellius turned back to her, his eyes burning with rage. His boot lashed out, catching her in the ribs. The impact sent her crashing to the ground, the air rushing from her lungs in a painful gasp. She curled in on herself, her body screaming in protest, but she refused to stay down.

The warriors’ cheers were deafening now, a cacophony of bloodthirsty delight. Nesta gritted her teeth, her vision swimming as she pushed herself up onto her knees.

Bellius loomed over her, his shadow falling across her like a shroud. “Stay down,” he growled.

Nesta spat blood onto the dirt. “No.”

She forced herself to her feet, her breath ragged, her ribs screaming in pain. Bellius circled her, his smirk widening. “Is this all you’ve got, little mouse?”

Nesta met his gaze, her voice a low, dangerous growl. “No. But it’s all *you* deserve.”

The crowd fell silent, the warriors watching in uneasy anticipation. Nesta didn’t look away from Bellius. She didn’t dare.

---

The world tilted, the ground rushing up to meet her before she could brace herself. Nesta’s knees hit the dirt with a jarring crack, her teeth snapping together so hard her jaw ached. The metallic tang of blood filled her mouth, copper and salt and something darker—something that tasted like defeat.

Bellius didn’t give her time to recover. His boot lashed out, catching her ribs with enough force to send her sprawling onto her back. The impact knocked the breath from her lungs in a wheezing gasp. Above her, the sky was a smear of gray, the clouds heavy with the promise of rain. Or maybe that was just the blood dripping into her eyes.

The warriors’ roars were a living thing, a wall of sound pressing in on her from all sides. Their voices were a chorus of hunger, of approval, of something far uglier. She could feel their eyes on her, could practically taste their excitement in the air. They wanted this. They wanted to see her broken.

Nesta spat a mouthful of blood onto the dirt, her vision swimming. Bellius loomed over her, his shadow blocking out the weak sunlight. His face was a mask of cold satisfaction, his lips curled into a sneer. “Yield,” he growled, the word a command, a taunt, a dare.

She bared her teeth at him, her fingers curling into the dirt as if she could claw her way back to her feet through sheer force of will. The pain was a white-hot brand searing through her side, her ribs screaming in protest with every shallow breath. But she wouldn’t yield. Not to him. Not to any of them.

“Never,” she rasped, the word barely more than a whisper. But it was enough.

Bellius’s expression darkened, his eyes narrowing into slits. His fist clenched at his side, the muscles in his arm tensing as he prepared to strike. The crowd held its breath, the air thick with the weight of what was to come.

Nesta didn’t look away. She wouldn’t give him the satisfaction.

A flash of crimson caught her eye, and she turned her head just enough to see Cassian stepping forward, his Siphons glowing like embers in the dim light. His wings were spread wide, his body coiled with restrained fury. For a heartbeat, she thought he might actually intervene. That he might put an end to this.

But then Devlon’s voice cut through the chaos, sharp and unyielding. “No interference.”

Cassian froze, his gaze locked on Bellius’s back. His jaw was clenched so tight she could see the muscle twitching beneath his skin. The Siphons on his knuckles pulsed with crimson light, as if they, too, were straining against the command.

Nesta’s chest ached. Not from the blows, not from the pain, but from the look on his face. The helplessness. The rage. The fear.

She knew that look. She’d seen it in the mirror every day for months.

Bellius’s fist hovered in the air above her, his knuckles white with the promise of violence. The crowd was silent now, the tension so thick it was suffocating. Nesta’s breath came in shallow, uneven gasps, her body trembling with the effort of staying conscious.

But she wouldn’t yield.

She wouldn’t.

---

The whistle cut through the air like a blade, sharp and sudden.

Nesta’s head snapped toward the sound, her vision swimming as she tried to focus. The training grounds had gone eerily still, the murmurs of the watching warriors dying into silence. Even the wind seemed to hold its breath.

Kars stood between her and Bellius, his broad frame blocking her view of the warrior. His face was unreadable, but his stance was absolute. No hesitation. No apology.

Bellius’s fist was still raised, his knuckles white with the force of his grip. His lip curled back in a snarl. *"Move."*

Kars didn’t flinch. *"Enough."*

The word was quiet, but it carried the weight of a command.

A ripple of shock moved through the crowd. Nesta could feel their stares, their unease. She forced herself to her feet, her legs unsteady beneath her, her breath ragged. Blood dripped from her split lip, but she didn’t wipe it away.

Cassian stepped forward, his wings half-spread, his crimson Siphons glowing faintly against his gloves. His voice was low, controlled. *"Kars is right. The match is over."*

Bellius’s gaze darkened, his jaw tightening. For a heartbeat, Nesta thought he might refuse. But then his fist lowered, his fingers uncurling with deliberate slowness. He stepped back, his boots kicking up dust as he retreated into the crowd.

The warriors parted for him, their eyes flickering between Kars and Nesta. Some looked relieved. Others looked furious.

Nesta’s gaze locked onto Kars. He turned to her, his expression unreadable, but his voice was quiet when he spoke. *"You fought well."*

She didn’t answer. Her chest burned, her body ached, but she refused to show it. Instead, her eyes flicked to Cassian.

He stood a few paces away, his face carefully blank, but his eyes—his eyes burned. Not with anger. Not with disappointment.

With something else. Something that looked like a question.

Behind them, Bellius melted into the crowd, his fists clenched at his sides. The warriors shifted, their murmurs starting up again, but Nesta didn’t hear them.

She only heard the silence between her and Cassian. The unspoken weight of it.

And the storm gathering on the horizon.

---

The hut was blessedly cool after the scorching sun of the training grounds, the air thick with the scent of sweat and damp earth. Nesta’s body ached in ways she hadn’t known possible—her muscles screaming, her bones heavy with exhaustion. She collapsed onto the narrow bed, the thin mattress doing little to cushion the impact. Her breath came in ragged gasps, her chest rising and falling too fast, too shallow.

*Stupid. So stupid.*

She had pushed too hard. Again. And for what? To prove she could? To prove she wasn’t weak?

The door creaked open, the sound like a blade dragging across stone. She didn’t need to look to know who it was.

Cassian stepped inside, his wings tucked tight against his back, his crimson Siphons glinting in the dim light. His face was a storm of its own—dark, furious, his jaw clenched so tight she could see the muscle feathering beneath his skin.

“You could have been killed.”

His voice was low, rough, the words barely more than a growl.

Nesta swallowed, her throat dry. She forced herself to meet his gaze, to lift her chin. “But I wasn’t.”

Cassian took a step closer, then another, until he loomed over her, his presence swallowing the small space. “What will it take for you to see that this isn’t a game?” His voice broke on the last word, raw and jagged.

Her chest tightened. She wanted to lash out, to snap at him, to drive him away before he could see the truth in her eyes. But the words lodged in her throat, tangled in the knot of emotion burning beneath her ribs.

Cassian’s gaze softened, just enough that she could see the pain beneath the anger. “Let me in, Nesta.”

The words were barely a whisper, but they struck her like a blow.

Her hands trembled. She wanted to say yes. Wanted to reach for him, to let him in, to let *herself* in. But the fear was a living thing inside her, coiling tight around her heart, her lungs, her throat.

Before she could respond, a shout echoed through the camp, sharp and urgent. They both turned toward the door, the moment shattered.

Cassian’s expression darkened. “Stay here.”

He was gone before she could protest, his wings cutting through the night like blades. The wind from his departure ruffled the loose strands of her hair, the chill of it seeping into her bones.

Nesta hesitated, her pulse pounding in her ears. Then, without thinking, she pushed off the bed and followed.

Her instincts drove her forward, into the darkness, toward the storm.

---

The wind howled through the Illyrian peaks, biting at Nesta’s exposed skin as she stepped into the training ring. The crowd of warriors parted for her, their murmurs dying into silence. She didn’t look at them. Didn’t acknowledge the way their gazes flicked between her and the center of the ring, where Bellius stood like a king surveying his domain.

His boot pressed into the chest of a young warrior, the boy’s face twisted in pain, his breath coming in ragged gasps. Nesta’s stomach twisted, but she didn’t slow. She didn’t think.

Bellius’s head lifted, his dark eyes locking onto hers. A slow, cruel smile spread across his face. *"This doesn’t concern you."*

She didn’t stop walking. Didn’t break stride. The gravel crunched beneath her boots, the only sound in the sudden hush. *"Get off him."*

The warriors around her shifted, their wings rustling like dry leaves. Bellius’s smirk widened, his grip tightening on the hilt of his dagger. *"Or what?"* His voice was a purr, mocking. *"You’ll save him like you saved the last one?"*

The words hit like a physical blow. The last one. The boy who had dared to speak to her, who had offered her a kind word in this gods-forsaken place. The boy Bellius had broken for it.

Nesta’s fingers curled into fists, her nails biting into her palms. She didn’t answer. Didn’t give him the satisfaction.

Then Cassian was there, stepping between them, his wings half-spread, his Siphons glowing like embers in the dim light. His voice was low, dangerous. *"Bellius. Stand down."*

Bellius didn’t even glance at him. His gaze remained fixed on Nesta, his boot still crushing the boy’s ribs. *"You’re not one of us,"* he said, his voice dripping with disdain. *"You never will be."*

Nesta didn’t flinch. Didn’t look away. *"And you’re a coward."*

The words hung in the air, sharp and final. The crowd inhaled as one, the silence so thick she could taste it.

Bellius’s face darkened, his fist raising.

Nesta braced herself, her breath shallow, her gaze unyielding.

The storm was coming—and it would change everything.
