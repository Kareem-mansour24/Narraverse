CHAPTER 1
The Weight of Wings

The wind howled like a living thing, clawing at Nesta’s skin as she clung to Cassian’s arms. She refused to look down. Refused to acknowledge the way her stomach lurched with every dip and rise of his wings, the way the cold bit through her gown as if it were nothing more than mist. The Illyrian Mountains loomed below, jagged and unforgiving, their peaks dusted with snow that glinted like shattered glass in the pale morning light.

She had not spoken a word since leaving Velaris. Not when Cassian had landed on her balcony, not when he had hauled her into the sky without so much as a warning. Not even when the wind had torn the breath from her lungs, leaving her gasping like a fish dragged from water.

The warriors below were watching.

Nesta could feel their eyes on her, heavy with disdain. They stood in clusters around the training grounds, their wings folded tight against their backs, their expressions ranging from open hostility to cold indifference. A few of them smirked. Others spat into the dirt as Cassian descended, as if her very presence was an insult.

She didn’t care. Let them stare. Let them hate her.

Cassian’s boots hit the ground with a thud, and he released her without ceremony, his grip on her arms lingering just long enough to ensure she wouldn’t crumple. Nesta staggered, her legs unsteady from the flight, but she caught herself before she could fall. She would not give them the satisfaction of seeing her weak.

“Home sweet home,” Cassian said, his voice rough with something she couldn’t place. Mockery, perhaps. Or pity.

Nesta said nothing. She turned her head slowly, taking in the stone huts dotting the mountainside, the crude training rings, the way the wind whipped through the narrow paths between them. It was a far cry from the warmth of Velaris, from the softness of her bed, the comfort of her books. But then, she had never belonged there, had she?

Cassian jerked his chin toward a small, squat structure near the edge of the camp. “That one’s yours.”

Nesta followed his gaze. The hut was little more than a pile of stones held together by moss and stubbornness. The door hung crooked on its hinges, and the single window was nothing more than a jagged hole in the wall, covered by a scrap of hide that fluttered weakly in the wind.

She lifted her chin. “Charming.”

Cassian’s mouth twitched, but he didn’t rise to the bait. Instead, he strode toward the hut, his wings rustling with the movement. Nesta trailed behind him, her skirts dragging in the dirt, her silence a weapon she wielded with precision.

The inside was worse.

The floor was packed earth, cold and uneven beneath her slippers. A narrow cot sat against one wall, its thin mattress sagging under the weight of a single wool blanket. A small table stood beside it, its surface scarred with knife marks, and a lone stool sat in the corner, its legs splintered. The air smelled of damp and old smoke, and the wind whistled through the cracks in the walls, carrying with it the scent of snow.

Nesta walked to the center of the room and turned in a slow circle, cataloging every flaw, every absence. No hearth. No lantern. No books. No warmth.

Cassian leaned against the doorframe, his arms crossed over his chest. “It’s not much,” he said, as if she hadn’t already noticed. “But it’s yours.”

Nesta said nothing. She crossed to the cot and pressed a finger into the mattress. It gave beneath her touch, the straw inside crunching softly. She withdrew her hand as if burned.

“You’ll be expected to train,” Cassian continued, his voice hardening. “Every day. Sunrise to sunset, unless I say otherwise.”

Nesta turned to face him, her expression blank. “And if I refuse?”

Cassian’s eyes flashed, the crimson of his Siphons flaring briefly. “Then you’ll starve. Or freeze. Or both.” He pushed off the doorframe and stepped inside, the space suddenly feeling much smaller with him in it. “This isn’t Velaris, Nesta. There are no second chances here. No one to clean up after you. No one to coddle you. You do what I say, when I say it, or you suffer the consequences.”

Nesta bared her teeth. “I’d rather die.”

Cassian’s laugh was sharp, humorless. “No, you wouldn’t.” He stepped closer, his boots scuffing against the dirt floor. “You’re a survivor, Nesta. Even when you don’t want to be. Even when you hate yourself for it.”

She wanted to strike him. Wanted to claw at his face, to make him bleed the way he had made her bleed, over and over again. But she didn’t. She stood there, her fingers curling into invisible claws at her sides, her breath coming fast and shallow.

Cassian studied her for a long moment, his gaze lingering on the hollows beneath her cheekbones, the way her gown hung loose on her frame. Then, without another word, he turned and walked out.

The door slammed shut behind him, the sound echoing through the hut like a gunshot.

Nesta stood in the sudden darkness, her breath visible in the frigid air. She didn’t move. Didn’t speak.

She simply waited.

For what, she didn’t know.

---

The first light of dawn had barely crept over the jagged peaks of Ramiel when the door to Cassian’s quarters groaned open. Nesta didn’t turn, didn’t acknowledge the brute as he stepped inside, his boots thudding against the rough-hewn floor. The scent of leather and snow clung to him, mingling with the sharp tang of the mountains.

“Get up,” he said, his voice low, rough.

She didn’t move. The thin blanket over her was little defense against the cold, but she refused to let him see her shiver.

A pause. Then the blanket was yanked away, the icy air biting at her skin. She bared her teeth, fingers curling into invisible claws. “Touch me again, and I’ll break your fingers.”

Cassian only smirked, his dark eyes glinting in the dim light. “You can try.” He tossed a bundle of clothes at her—leather, like his, but sized for someone smaller. “Put those on. You’ve got five minutes.”

Nesta didn’t reach for them. “Or what?”

His wings rustled as he crossed his arms. “Or I’ll drag you out there in that pretty gown of yours.”

She wanted to scream. Wanted to claw at his face, to make him bleed. But the hollowness inside her swallowed the urge, leaving only a dull, simmering rage. She snatched the clothes and turned away, stripping off the gown with sharp, jerky movements.

The leather was stiff, unfamiliar. It hugged her too tightly, the scent of it cloying. She braided her hair back with practiced efficiency, the familiar motion the only thing keeping her from lashing out.

Cassian was waiting by the door when she turned, his arms crossed, his Siphons glinting crimson in the morning light. “Let’s go.”

She didn’t speak as they stepped outside, the wind howling down from the peaks. The training grounds were already alive with movement—warriors stretching, sparring, their laughter sharp and mocking. They fell silent as she and Cassian approached, their eyes raking over her with open disdain.

At the center of the ring stood Devlon, his scarred face twisted into a sneer. “What’s this, Cassian? Bringing your pet to play?”

Nesta’s jaw tightened, but she said nothing.

Cassian ignored the jab. “Nesta’s training with you lot. Starting today.”

A ripple of laughter ran through the warriors. One of them—a hulking male with a broken nose—stepped forward, his grin all teeth. “You hear that, lads? The lady’s come to learn how to fight.”

Devlon’s smile was a blade. “Who’s she paired with, then?”

Cassian’s gaze flicked over the crowd before landing on the broken-nosed warrior. “Bellius.”

The male’s grin widened. “Delighted.”

Nesta didn’t look at him. Didn’t give him the satisfaction.

Cassian clapped a hand on her shoulder, his grip firm. “Don’t hold back,” he muttered, low enough for only her to hear. “And don’t expect them to, either.”

She shrugged him off, stepping into the ring. The warriors formed a loose circle around them, their jeers and catcalls rising like a storm. Bellius cracked his knuckles, his eyes gleaming with anticipation.

“Ready to dance, princess?”

She didn’t answer.

He lunged.

One moment, she was standing there, her body rigid with fury. The next, his fist was flying toward her face. She flinched—just once, just enough for the movement to betray her.

And then the world narrowed to the space between them, to the promise of pain.

---

The snow beneath Nesta’s cheek was cold, wet. The laughter of the warriors above her was anything but.

She tasted iron. Blood. Her blood.

Her lungs burned as she dragged in a ragged breath, the impact of Bellius’s boot still reverberating through her ribs. The world spun, just for a heartbeat, before she forced herself to focus. To push up onto her hands, her arms trembling with the effort.

The laughter died as Cassian’s voice cut through the morning air, sharp as a blade. *"Enough."*

Bellius didn’t even have the decency to look ashamed. He just smirked, wiping his mouth with the back of his hand before stepping back. The other warriors shifted, their amusement fading into something more cautious, more watchful.

Nesta didn’t look at them. Didn’t give them the satisfaction.

Her gaze locked onto Cassian.

For a heartbeat, his dark eyes held something she didn’t expect. Regret. Maybe even guilt. But then it was gone, replaced by the hard, unyielding mask of the warrior she knew too well.

She spat blood onto the snow between them, the red stark against the white.

His hand was already outstretched, offering to help her up.

Nesta ignored it.

Her fingers curled into the snow, her nails biting into her palms as she pushed herself to her feet. The world tilted, just for a second, but she steadied herself, her breath coming in shallow, controlled gasps.

Cassian’s jaw tightened. *"Nesta—"*

She didn’t let him finish. Didn’t let him say whatever useless, empty words he had prepared. She turned on her heel and walked away, her boots sinking into the snow with each step.

The first snowflakes of the coming storm dusted her hair, clinging to the dark strands like tiny, frozen stars. She didn’t brush them away.

Behind her, the warriors were silent. Even Bellius had the sense to keep his mouth shut.

Cassian didn’t call after her.

She didn’t expect him to.
