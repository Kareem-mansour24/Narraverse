CHAPTER 15
The Breaking Point

The predawn air was thin and brittle, biting at Nesta’s lungs as she woke. Her body ached—a deep, throbbing protest in her bones, her muscles stiff from yesterday’s sparring. She had slept in her clothes, the gray gown now rumpled, the fabric clinging to her sweat-damp skin. The bruises along her ribs and arms were dark smudges beneath the pale light filtering through the tent flaps.

She didn’t bother to stretch. Didn’t bother to check the mirror. There was no point.

Outside, the camp was already stirring, the low murmur of voices carrying across the rocky ground. She stepped into the cold, her breath curling in front of her, and moved through the motions of her morning routine with mechanical precision. Wash. Braid. Ignore the way her hands trembled as she tied back her hair.

The warriors watched her. She could feel their gazes like brands against her skin, some curious, some mocking, all of them hungry. And then there was the boy—Bellius—his eyes still lingering on her from yesterday, wide and grateful. She had knocked him flat on his back during their match, and he had looked at her like she was something to be revered.

She ignored him, too.

Devlon stood at the center of the training grounds, his massive arms crossed over his chest, his scarred face split into a grin that didn’t reach his eyes. “Today,” he announced, his voice booming, “we test endurance.”

The warriors shifted, their wings rustling, their laughter sharp and cruel.

Nesta didn’t react. She had learned not to.

“You’ll carry a boulder up the mountain,” Devlon continued, jerking his chin toward the jagged peak looming over them. “While my warriors fly overhead.” He gestured to the sky, where a few of them were already circling, their dark forms cutting through the gray light. “And they’ll be throwing rocks at you.”

A few of the warriors chuckled, the sound like gravel underfoot. Kars, a hulking brute with a broken nose, smirked at her, his teeth yellowed. “Hope you’ve got a strong back, girl.”

Nesta said nothing. She didn’t look at him.

Devlon pointed to the largest boulder at the edge of the training grounds, its surface rough and jagged. “That one.”

She walked toward it, her boots crunching over the frost-kissed earth. The weight of their stares pressed down on her, but she didn’t falter. She crouched, sliding her arms around the boulder, her fingers digging into the cold stone.

It was heavier than it looked.

Her muscles screamed as she lifted, her legs shaking with the effort. The boulder scraped against her chest, the rough edges biting into her skin. She gritted her teeth and took a step forward.

Then another.

The first rock struck her shoulder before she had gone ten paces.

It wasn’t large—just a fist-sized chunk of stone—but it hit with enough force to make her stumble. Pain flared, bright and hot, and she nearly dropped the boulder.

Laughter erupted around her, sharp and mocking. She didn’t look up. Didn’t give them the satisfaction.

She adjusted her grip, her fingers slipping on the cold stone, and kept walking.

Another rock. This one hit her thigh. Then her back. Then her arm.

The warriors’ laughter echoed around her, bouncing off the cliffs, filling the air like a chorus of jeers. She didn’t stop. Didn’t slow.

She just walked.

---

The boulder was a living thing, a beast of stone and malice that had latched onto her back and refused to let go. Nesta’s thighs burned, her lungs screaming with every ragged breath as she forced herself up the mountain path. The Illyrian warriors had taken to the skies the moment the test began, their wings cutting through the crisp morning air with effortless grace. They soared above her, a storm of feathers and mockery.

*"Look at the High Lady’s sister, crawling like a worm!"*

The taunts rained down, sharp as the rocks they hurled. Nesta didn’t look up. She couldn’t afford to. The boulder demanded every scrap of her focus, her balance, her strength. One wrong step, one misplaced foot, and it would roll back down the mountain, crushing her beneath its weight.

A shadow passed over her, too close, too deliberate. She barely had time to flinch before a rock struck her temple. Pain exploded behind her eyes, white-hot and blinding. Her knees buckled, but she caught herself, fingers digging into the dirt to keep from collapsing. The boulder wobbled, threatening to escape her grip, but she locked her arms around it, teeth gritted against the agony radiating through her skull.

*"Pathetic."*

Bellius’s voice cut through the haze of pain. She didn’t need to look to know it was him. His laughter was unmistakable, cruel and smug. Another rock struck her ribs, stealing her breath. She gasped, vision swimming, but she didn’t fall. Not yet.

The warriors above her whooped, their jeers growing louder, more vicious. They circled like vultures, waiting for her to break. Nesta’s fingers curled into the dirt, her nails splitting against the rough earth. She wouldn’t give them the satisfaction. She *wouldn’t*.

A low growl rumbled through the air, barely audible over the wind and the warriors’ taunts. Nesta didn’t dare look toward the sound, but she knew—*Cassian*. His presence was a storm at her back, a silent, seething fury she could feel even from a distance. His hands were clenched at his sides, his knuckles white beneath the crimson glow of his Siphons. His jaw was tight, his wings half-spread as if he were fighting the urge to launch himself into the fray.

She hated that she noticed. Hated that some traitorous part of her wanted to look at him, to see if his eyes were burning with the same rage she felt coiled in her own chest.

*"You’re wasting your time, Archeron!"* Bellius’s voice rang out, dripping with amusement. *"The mountain doesn’t want you. The sky doesn’t want you. No one does."*

The words struck deeper than any rock. Nesta’s breath hitched, her vision blurring as another projectile found its mark, this time against her ribs. Pain lanced through her, sharp and unrelenting. Her grip on the boulder faltered.

And then she was falling.

Her knee hit the ground first, the impact sending a jolt of agony up her leg. The boulder rolled free, tumbling down the slope with a thunderous crack. Nesta didn’t try to stop it. She couldn’t. Her arms trembled, her body refusing to obey.

Laughter erupted around her, a chorus of triumph. She didn’t look up. She didn’t need to. She knew what she’d see—Bellius, landing in front of her with that smug, vicious smirk, his wings flaring wide as if to emphasize just how far above her he was.

*"Pathetic,"* he spat, his voice dripping with satisfaction.

Nesta’s fingers curled into the dirt, her breath coming in ragged gasps. She didn’t have the strength to stand. Didn’t have the strength to do anything but kneel there, broken and bleeding, while the world laughed.

---

The laughter died as suddenly as it had begun.

Nesta didn’t look up. Didn’t need to. She knew that voice—knew the command in it, the barely leashed fury. Cassian.

“Bellius.” His name was a whip-crack across the training grounds.

The warrior didn’t so much as flinch. “General,” he drawled, not turning. His boot nudged her side, not hard enough to bruise, but enough to make her ribs scream. “Just teaching the lady some humility.”

Nesta forced her head up. Forced her body to obey, though every movement sent fire lancing through her. The world tilted, but she locked her knees, refusing to crumple again. The warriors had gone quiet, their amusement bleeding into something else—something like wariness.

Bellius was still smiling, but his eyes were cold. “You Illyrians are all the same,” he said, loud enough for the others to hear. “Think you can order us around, even when you’re not in uniform.” His gaze flicked to Cassian’s wings, the crimson Siphons glowing like embers at his knuckles. “But some of us remember what it means to be Illyrian. What it means to be *strong*.”

Cassian didn’t move. Didn’t even breathe. But the air between them was a live wire, humming with the promise of violence.

Nesta bared her teeth. She didn’t speak. Didn’t need to.

Bellius’s smirk faltered.

She had spent months feeling nothing—nothing but the hollow ache of emptiness, the numbness that had swallowed her whole. But now, as she met his gaze, something flickered in her chest. Not defiance. Not pride.

Rage.

Pure, white-hot rage.

It burned through the exhaustion, the pain, the suffocating weight of her own helplessness. And in that moment, she didn’t care if she was broken. Didn’t care if she was bleeding.

She would not kneel.

Bellius must have seen it in her eyes, because his smile twisted into something uglier. “What’s the matter, Archeron? Cat got your tongue?”

Cassian’s wings flared, the leather creaking like a war drum. “Step. Back.”

Bellius didn’t. He leaned in instead, close enough that she could smell the sour tang of his breath. “Or what, General? You’ll punish me for teaching your *pet* a lesson?”

The word was a spark to tinder.

Nesta lunged.

It was a clumsy, graceless thing—her body still screaming, her muscles trembling—but her fingers curled into claws, and she went for his throat.

Bellius backhanded her before she could connect.

The world exploded in stars. She hit the ground hard, her cheekbone singing, but she was already scrambling up, already snarling—

“Enough.”

Cassian’s voice was ice. Frost. The kind of cold that burned.

Bellius finally turned, his face a mask of false innocence. “General. I was just—”

“You were just leaving.” Cassian’s Siphons pulsed, crimson light bleeding into the air. “Now.”

For a heartbeat, no one moved. Then Bellius laughed, a sharp, ugly sound. “Fine. But this isn’t over.” His gaze flicked to Nesta, lingering just long enough to make her skin crawl. “Not by a long shot.”

Then he was gone, launching himself into the sky with a powerful beat of his wings.

The silence he left behind was deafening.

Nesta didn’t look at Cassian. Didn’t look at the warriors, who were suddenly very interested in their boots, their weapons, the sky—anywhere but at her.

She wiped the blood from her split lip with the back of her hand and forced herself to stand straight. To breathe.

Cassian’s gaze burned into her, heavy and unreadable.

She didn’t care.

She was hollow. She was broken.

But she was still standing.

---

The wind howled across the training grounds, biting through the thin fabric of Nesta’s tunic. She didn’t flinch. The cold was nothing compared to the ice in her veins, the hollow ache in her chest that had become as familiar as her own breath.

Cassian’s voice cut through the gale. “Nesta.”

She didn’t turn. Didn’t acknowledge him. The other warriors had already dispersed, their laughter and grumbles fading into the distance. Only Cassian remained, his wings slightly ruffled from the wind, his Siphons glinting like drops of blood in the fading light.

“You pushed too hard today,” he said, stepping closer. His voice was low, rough. “You’re going to break something.”

Nesta bared her teeth. “I didn’t ask for your concern.”

“No,” he agreed, his jaw tightening. “You never do.”

She whirled on him then, her braid whipping over her shoulder. “Then why are you still here? Why do you keep following me like some lost pup?”

His eyes darkened, the gold in them turning molten. “Because someone has to make sure you don’t kill yourself in the process.”

“Maybe I *want* to,” she snapped, the words tearing from her throat before she could stop them. The truth of them burned like acid.

Cassian went very still. Then, slowly, he stepped closer, close enough that she could see the flecks of amber in his irises, close enough that she could feel the heat radiating off him. “You think I don’t see what you’re doing?” His voice was raw, barely above a growl. “You’re not just fighting them. You’re fighting *me*. And you’re losing.”

Her breath hitched. The wind roared in her ears, but it was nothing compared to the storm inside her. She wanted to scream. She wanted to strike him. She wanted to—

But the words died on her lips.

Cassian’s gaze dropped to her mouth, then back to her eyes. His expression was unreadable, but his hands flexed at his sides, as if he were fighting the urge to reach for her.

Nesta turned away before he could see the crack in her armor. Her boots crunched over the frost-kissed rocks as she strode toward the cliffs, the wind tugging at her clothes, her hair. She didn’t look back.

“I don’t need you,” she whispered, the words barely audible over the howl of the wind.

Behind her, Cassian didn’t move. Didn’t speak.

And when she finally reached the edge of the cliff, the vast expanse of the mountains stretching before her, she didn’t let herself wonder if he was still watching.

---

The hut smelled of damp wool and old smoke. Nesta shoved the door open with her shoulder, her muscles screaming in protest. Every step had been a lesson in agony, her body a map of bruises and strain. She didn’t bother to hide it. Let them see what they’d made of her.

Kars was already inside, perched on the edge of the narrow cot like he’d been waiting for hours. Maybe he had. His dark eyes flicked over her as she limped in, but he didn’t rise. Didn’t speak. Just held up a jar of salve and a roll of bandages, the gesture so quiet it made her chest tighten.

Nesta exhaled through her nose. She should tell him to leave. Should insist she didn’t need help, didn’t need anything from anyone. But the exhaustion won, dragging her down like a riptide. She sank onto the cot beside him, her legs trembling with the effort of holding herself upright.

Kars uncorked the jar. The scent of mint and something earthy filled the air between them. His hands were steady as he scooped out a dollop of salve, the callouses on his fingers rough against her skin when he reached for her arm. Nesta flinched.

“You’re not weak,” he said, voice low. His thumb pressed into the worst of the bruising along her forearm, and she bit back a hiss. “But you’re fighting the wrong battle.”

Her head snapped up. “What’s the right one?”

Kars didn’t look at her. Just kept working, his touch careful, methodical. “Learning to trust someone other than yourself.”

The words landed like a blow. Nesta bared her teeth. “Trust gets you killed.”

“Or it keeps you alive.” He finally met her gaze, his own unreadable. “You think you’re the only one who’s lost everything? Who’s had to claw their way back from the dark?”

She wanted to laugh. Wanted to shove him away and tell him he didn’t know the first thing about her. But the salve was cool against her skin, soothing the fire beneath it, and his hands were warm, and for the first time in months, she didn’t feel entirely alone.

Her fingers trembled as she reached for the jar, taking it from him. “Why do you care?”

Kars’s expression didn’t change. “Because someone has to.”

---

The cold bit at Nesta’s skin the moment she stepped outside, the wind howling through the Illyrian peaks like a living thing. She welcomed it—the sting of it, the way it numbed her fingers and cheeks, the way it made her lungs ache with every breath. Anything to distract from the storm inside her.

She didn’t know how long she walked, only that her feet carried her toward the cliffs, where the world dropped away into nothingness. The stars above were sharp and bright, indifferent. She hated them for it.

“You’ll freeze to death out here.”

The voice was low, rough with exhaustion. Cassian.

Nesta didn’t turn. She could feel him behind her, the heat of his body a stark contrast to the winter air. His wings were folded tightly against his back, the crimson glow of his Siphons muted in the dark.

“Go away,” she said.

He didn’t. Of course he didn’t.

Instead, he stepped beside her, close enough that she could feel the brush of his arm against hers, but not close enough to touch. His gaze was fixed on the horizon, where the sky bled into the black of the mountains.

Silence stretched between them, thick and suffocating. Nesta’s fingers curled into fists, her nails digging into her palms. She wanted to scream. She wanted to shatter the quiet.

“Why do you stay?” The words slipped out before she could stop them, barely audible over the wind.

Cassian turned his head, and when his eyes met hers, they burned. Not with anger. Not with pity. With something far worse.

Something like understanding.

“Because I see you, Nesta,” he said, his voice steady, sure. “And I’m not going anywhere.”

Her breath hitched. The words struck her like a physical blow, knocking the air from her lungs. She wanted to laugh. She wanted to sneer. *See her?* No one saw her. No one wanted to.

But Cassian—stubborn, infuriating Cassian—stood there, unflinching, as if he truly believed what he’d said.

Her chest tightened, her throat closing around the words she wanted to hurl at him. *Leave me alone. Stop pretending. Stop caring.*

But they wouldn’t come.

Instead, she turned to him, her voice barely a whisper, raw with something she refused to name.

“What if I don’t want you to see me?”

---

The wind howled through the Illyrian peaks, biting at Nesta’s cheeks as she stood frozen in the shadow of the training ring. Cassian’s wings were still outstretched from his landing, the crimson glow of his Siphons pulsing like embers in the dark. His jaw was tight, his voice rough as gravel when he spoke.

“Then you’ll keep fighting. And you’ll keep losing.”

Her hands clenched into fists, nails digging into her palms until she could feel the sting of half-moons pressed into her skin. She wanted to snap at him, to hurl words like knives—*What do you know about losing?* But the truth of it lodged in her throat, thick and suffocating.

Before she could retort, a shout split the night.

Cassian’s head whipped toward the sound, his body tensing. The training grounds were alive with movement, warriors gathering in a tight knot, their voices rising in a chorus of anger. His wings flared wide, the wind from their span kicking up dust at her feet.

“Stay here,” he ordered, already turning.

Nesta didn’t think. Didn’t *let* herself think.

She followed.

Her boots pounded against the rocky earth as she trailed him, the cold air burning in her lungs. The crowd ahead thickened, a wall of leather and muscle, and Cassian’s wings cut through them like a blade. Nesta shoved her way after him, elbows sharp, ignoring the curses thrown her way.

Then she saw.

Bellius stood at the center of the circle, his boot pressed into the chest of a young warrior. The boy’s face was pale, his breath coming in ragged gasps, his fingers clawing at the ground. A training sword lay discarded beside him, its blade snapped in two.

Bellius smirked, his voice dripping with false pity. “Looks like someone else can’t handle the grind.”

Nesta’s vision narrowed to a pinprick of white-hot fury.

---

The world had sharpened to a blade’s edge, every sound and movement slicing through the haze of her fury. The young warrior was still on the ground, gasping for breath, his face pressed into the dirt. Bellius loomed over him, his boot planted on the boy’s back, grinding him down like he was nothing more than refuse.

Nesta’s fingers twitched at her sides, her nails biting into her palms. The warriors around them had gone quiet, their murmurs dying into something heavier, something charged. She didn’t care. She didn’t care about their whispers or their judgment or the way they looked at her like she was a problem to be solved.

“Get off him.”

The words came out low, sharp. A command.

Bellius turned slowly, his smirk widening as he took her in. “Or what?” His voice was a drawl, dripping with amusement. “You’ll save him like you saved the last one?”

The words hit like a physical blow, but Nesta didn’t flinch. She could feel the weight of the warriors’ stares, the way their eyes flicked between her and Bellius, waiting. Waiting to see what she’d do. Waiting to see if she’d break.

She didn’t.

“You’re not one of us,” Bellius continued, stepping away from the boy, his gaze raking over her like she was something foul. “You never will be.”

Nesta bared her teeth. “And you’re a coward.”

The smirk faltered. Just for a second. Just long enough for her to see the flicker of rage in his eyes.

Then Cassian was there, stepping between them, his voice a low growl. “Bellius. Stand down.”

Bellius ignored him. Of course he did. Because this wasn’t about the boy on the ground anymore. This was about her. About the way she didn’t belong. About the way she refused to bend.

“You think you’re so tough,” Bellius sneered, taking a step closer. “But you’re nothing. Just a human playing at being Illyrian.”

Nesta didn’t move. Didn’t blink. “And you’re still talking.”

The warriors around them shifted, some of them snickering, others watching with something like unease. She could feel their eyes on her, could feel the weight of their expectations. But she didn’t care. She didn’t care about any of it.

Bellius’s face twisted, his smirk fading into something uglier. “You—”

He lunged.

Nesta saw it coming—the way his body coiled, the way his fist flew toward her face. She didn’t think. She moved.

She sidestepped, her body turning fluid, instinct taking over. Bellius’s momentum carried him past her, and she drove her knee into his gut with everything she had. The air left his lungs in a whoosh, and he doubled over, staggering back before collapsing onto the dirt.

Silence.

Then the murmurs started again, louder this time, the warriors’ voices overlapping as they reacted. Some of them were laughing. Others were staring at her like they’d never seen her before.

Nesta didn’t look at any of them. She didn’t look at Cassian, either, though she could feel the weight of his gaze on her, heavy and unreadable.

Bellius groaned from the ground, his hands clutching his stomach. “You—”

“Stay down,” Nesta said, her voice flat. “Or I’ll put you down again.”

And then she turned and walked away, her boots kicking up dust as she left the training grounds behind.

---

The silence that followed was thicker than the mountain mist, pressing down on the gathered warriors like a physical weight. Nesta’s chest heaved, her breath coming in sharp, controlled bursts as she stood over Bellius. The male gasped, his face twisted in a grimace of pain and humiliation, his hands trembling as he pushed himself up from the dirt.

She didn’t look away. Didn’t flinch. The warriors around them had gone still, their usual jeers and taunts dying on their lips as they watched the scene unfold. Nesta’s fingers twitched at her sides, still curled as if they held the invisible claws she’d used to tear into Bellius’s wings. The memory of his scream echoed in her ears, but she didn’t regret it. Not for a second.

A shadow fell over her, and she didn’t need to look to know it was Cassian. His presence was a warmth at her back, a steadying force even when she didn’t want it. His voice was low, barely more than a rumble. *“Enough.”*

Bellius’s lips peeled back from his teeth in a snarl. “This isn’t over.” His voice was raw, scraped from his throat like gravel. Without another word, he shoved past the warriors, their bodies parting instinctively to let him through. The night swallowed him whole, his retreating form vanishing into the darkness beyond the torchlight.

Nesta exhaled through her nose, her gaze still fixed on the spot where he’d disappeared. Then, movement at her feet. The young warrior—Gwyn, was it?—pushed himself up on one elbow, his face pale but his eyes clear. “Thank you,” he rasped.

She didn’t answer. Didn’t know what to say. The words felt too heavy, too *real*, and she wasn’t ready to carry them.

Cassian shifted beside her, his wings rustling as he turned to face her fully. The crimson glow of his Siphons pulsed faintly in the dim light, casting shadows across the sharp angles of his face. “You didn’t have to do that,” he said quietly.

Nesta’s head snapped toward him, her teeth bared. “I didn’t *ask* you to intervene.”

His jaw tightened, but his voice remained even. “I know.”

“Then why are you still standing here?” The words came out sharper than she intended, laced with something brittle and raw. She didn’t want him here. Didn’t want his quiet understanding or his stupid, infuriating patience. She wanted to be left alone, to let the hollow space inside her expand until it swallowed everything else.

Cassian’s gaze burned into hers, unyielding. “Because you don’t have to fight them alone.”

The air between them crackled, charged with something she refused to name. The warriors’ murmurs rose around them, a low hum of shock and grudging respect, but Nesta barely heard them. All she could focus on was the male in front of her—the male who had seen her at her worst, who had never once looked away.

She opened her mouth to tell him exactly where he could shove his concern, but the words died on her tongue. Instead, she turned away, her boots kicking up dust as she strode toward the cliffs, the wind howling in her ears like a challenge.
