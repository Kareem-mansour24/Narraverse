CHAPTER 6
The Silence Between Blows

The drums woke her before dawn.

Nesta lay still in the narrow cot, listening to the rhythmic thudding that vibrated through the wooden walls of the hut, through the bones of the mountain itself. The scent of smoke curled through the cracks—acrid, biting. Not the warm hearth-smoke of home, but the harsh tang of warriors’ fires, the kind that stung the eyes and clung to the back of the throat.

She exhaled through her nose, slow and measured, and catalogued the pain.

Her ribs ached where Bellius had driven his fist into them yesterday. The bruise on her thigh throbbed with every heartbeat, a deep, insistent pulse. Her knuckles were split, the skin raw from where she’d struck the wooden training dummy until her hands bled. And her back—

She refused to think of her back.

Nesta sat up, the thin wool blanket pooling around her waist. The hut was cold, the kind of cold that seeped into the marrow, but she didn’t shiver. She never shivered anymore. Not since the war. Not since the hole inside her had opened so wide there was no room left for anything as frivolous as discomfort.

She dressed quickly, pulling on the leather training gear that had been left for her—stiff, unyielding, the scent of sweat and old blood clinging to the fabric. The laces of her boots bit into her fingers as she tied them, but she didn’t flinch. Pain was just another language now, one she was learning to speak fluently.

Outside, the camp was already alive. Warriors moved in loose formations, their wings half-furled as they stretched and warmed their muscles. The air was thick with the sound of steel on steel, the grunts of exertion, the occasional bark of laughter. Nesta ignored it all, her gaze fixed on the path ahead.

She didn’t see Cassian until he fell into step beside her.

“You look like hell,” he said, voice rough with sleep.

Nesta didn’t glance at him. “Go away.”

A muscle feathered in his jaw, but he didn’t leave. “Devlon’s got something new planned for today. Something involving cliffs.”

She said nothing.

Cassian exhaled sharply through his nose. “Nesta—”

“Don’t.” The word was a blade, honed and precise. She didn’t look at him, didn’t slow her pace. “I don’t want to hear it.”

He was silent for a long moment. Then, quietly, “You can’t keep doing this.”

She bared her teeth. “Watch me.”

The training grounds were already crowded when they arrived. Warriors stood in loose clusters, their wings rustling in the cold morning wind. At the center of it all, Devlon stood with his arms crossed, his scarred face split in a grin that didn’t reach his eyes.

Nesta stopped at the edge of the gathering, her hands loose at her sides. She didn’t look at Cassian. Didn’t look at anyone.

Devlon’s gaze found her immediately. “Ah, Archeron. Right on time.” His grin widened. “Today’s test is simple. Balance. Precision.” He gestured to the cliffs behind him, where narrow paths wound along the sheer rock face, barely wide enough for a single person to walk. “You’ll be paired with Bellius.”

A ripple of amusement moved through the crowd. Nesta didn’t react.

Bellius stepped forward, his smirk already in place. He was a brute of a male, all corded muscle and sharp teeth, his wings marked with the scars of a hundred battles. He’d made it clear from the first day that he didn’t think she belonged here. That she was weak. That she would break.

Nesta met his gaze, her expression blank.

Devlon clapped his hands. “Begin when you’re ready.”

The warriors fell silent, watching.

Nesta turned toward the cliffs. The wind tugged at her braid, pulling strands loose to whip across her face. She didn’t brush them away.

The path was narrow, barely more than a ledge carved into the rock. One misstep, and she’d plummet. One wrong move, and she’d be nothing.

She stepped onto it.

The wind howled in her ears, sharp and cold. Her boots scraped against the stone, the rough surface biting into her soles. Behind her, Bellius followed, his smirk never wavering.

Nesta didn’t look down. Didn’t look back.

She walked.

---

The wind howled across the cliffs, sharp as a blade and twice as cold. It tugged at the braided coronet of Nesta’s hair, as if trying to unravel her piece by piece. Below, the Illyrian camp sprawled like a dark stain against the snow, the warriors little more than shifting shadows in the predawn gloom. She could feel their eyes on her—judging, mocking, waiting for her to fail.

She wouldn’t.

The path narrowed ahead, a treacherous ribbon of ice and stone clinging to the mountainside. Nesta stepped onto it without hesitation, her boots finding purchase on the slick surface. The cold bit through the thin soles, but she ignored it. Ignored the way her breath fogged in the air, ignored the way her pulse thrummed in her throat.

Behind her, Bellius’s voice slithered through the wind. *"Scared, little mouse?"* His laugh was a low, guttural thing. *"One wrong step and you’ll be nothing but a stain on the rocks."*

Nesta didn’t turn. Didn’t flinch. She had spent months learning to silence the noise in her head—the fear, the doubt, the endless, gnawing emptiness. She had carved it out of herself, piece by piece, until there was only this: the next step, the next breath, the next moment.

Her boot slipped.

For one heart-stopping second, her stomach lurched as the world tilted beneath her. Then her fingers dug into the rock face, her muscles locking as she steadied herself. The wind screamed in her ears, but she didn’t make a sound.

Bellius’s laughter rang out, sharp and mocking. *"Careful, Archeron. Wouldn’t want to disappoint the warlord by dying before breakfast."*

Nesta exhaled through her nose, slow and controlled. She could feel Cassian’s gaze on her like a brand, heavy and unreadable. He stood below, arms crossed over his chest, his wings half-furled against the cold. She refused to look at him. Refused to give him the satisfaction of seeing her struggle.

The path widened slightly, and she quickened her pace, her breath steady despite the altitude. The ledge ahead was narrow, barely more than a shelf of rock jutting over the abyss. She reached it and turned, her back to the drop, her eyes locking onto Bellius.

He grinned, slow and vicious. *"What’s the matter, mouse? Cat got your tongue?"*

Nesta tilted her head. *"You talk too much."*

Bellius’s grin faltered.

She didn’t give him time to recover. Her boot lashed out, striking a loose rock at the edge of the path. It skittered down the cliffside, gaining speed before it slammed into Bellius’s chest with a sickening *thud*.

The warrior staggered, his face twisting in shock and rage. His arms windmilled as he fought for balance, but the damage was done. The rock had struck true, knocking the breath from his lungs.

Below, the warriors erupted into murmurs, a ripple of surprise and approval cutting through the cold. Nesta didn’t wait to see Bellius’s reaction. She turned on her heel and walked away, her steps sure, her spine straight.

The wind howled in her wake, but she didn’t hear it.

She only heard the silence between blows.

---

The path was slick with dew, the morning light casting long shadows across the jagged rocks. Nesta’s boots crunched over loose stones as she pivoted, her breath steady despite the weight pressing against her ribs. Bellius didn’t give her time to recover. His fist lashed out again, and she barely twisted away in time, the wind of his strike ruffling the loose strands of her braided hair.

The warriors below had gone quiet. No more jeers, no more laughter. Just the heavy silence of men watching something they hadn’t expected.

Nesta bared her teeth. She wasn’t some helpless thing to be toyed with. Not anymore.

Bellius lunged, his movements sloppy with overconfidence. She sidestepped, letting him stumble past her before driving her elbow into the space between his shoulder blades. He grunted, but the path was too narrow for him to turn easily. She used it. Pressed her advantage. Her knee snapped up into his gut, and he doubled over with a wheeze.

For a heartbeat, she thought she had him.

Then his hand shot out, fingers closing around her ankle. She barely had time to brace before he yanked, sending her crashing onto her back. The impact knocked the breath from her lungs, her vision swimming as pain flared along her spine. Bellius loomed over her, his face twisted in a snarl.

“Pathetic,” he spat.

Nesta didn’t answer. She rolled, kicking out with her free leg. Her heel connected with his knee, and he staggered, his grip on her ankle loosening just enough for her to wrench free. She scrambled to her feet, her ribs screaming in protest, but she didn’t dare show it.

The path narrowed further ahead, the drop to the training grounds below sheer and unforgiving. Bellius smirked, thinking he had her trapped.

He didn’t.

Nesta feinted left, then drove her shoulder into his chest when he moved to block her. He grunted, his arms flailing as he teetered on the edge. For a second, she thought he might go over. Then his hand shot out, gripping her wrist in a bruising hold.

She didn’t hesitate. Her free hand balled into a fist, and she slammed it into his jaw. Bone cracked against bone, and Bellius’s head snapped to the side. He staggered, his grip slipping—

“Enough.”

Cassian’s voice cut through the air like a blade.

Bellius froze, his chest heaving. Nesta didn’t.

She drove her knee into his stomach, putting all her weight behind the strike. He folded like parchment, collapsing onto the path with a choked gasp. The warriors below erupted into cheers, but Nesta barely heard them. Her focus was on Bellius, on the way his face contorted with humiliation as he glared up at her.

She didn’t smile. Didn’t gloat.

She just stood there, her breath steady, her hands trembling at her sides.

Then Cassian landed beside them, his wings flaring wide before tucking neatly against his back. His crimson Siphons glowed faintly, a warning. He stepped between her and Bellius, his voice low, dangerous.

“You’ve made your point.”

Bellius spat blood onto the rocks, his eyes burning with fury. But he didn’t argue. Didn’t challenge. He just pushed to his feet and stalked away, the warriors parting to let him pass like a storm moving through their ranks.

Nesta watched him go, her pulse thundering in her ears.

She had won.

And yet, as Cassian turned to face her, his expression unreadable, she couldn’t shake the feeling that the fight had only just begun.

---

The hut smelled of damp wool and iron, the scent of blood and sweat clinging to the air like a second skin. Nesta’s body throbbed in time with her heartbeat, each breath a knife between her ribs. She hadn’t bothered to wipe the dirt from her face, the grime from her hands. What was the point? It would only return tomorrow.

She collapsed onto the pallet, the rough fabric scraping against her raw knuckles. The fight still rang in her bones, the impact of fist against flesh, the way the ground had rushed up to meet her again and again. And yet—

She had won.

The thought should have been a balm, but it settled like a stone in her gut. Cassian’s face flashed behind her eyelids, that unreadable mask he wore, the way his wings had flared just slightly as he’d turned away. As if she were something to be endured.

The door creaked.

Nesta didn’t open her eyes. She knew the sound of Kars’s footsteps, the way he moved like a shadow, quiet and deliberate. The scent of herbs and clean linen preceded him, and then the rustle of bandages being unwound.

“You did well today.”

His voice was low, careful. Nesta kept her eyes shut, her jaw tight. “I didn’t need your approval.”

A pause. Then the brush of his fingers against her side, gentle as he lifted the edge of her tunic to inspect the bruising. She flinched, more from the contact than the pain.

“I know.” The bandages whispered against her skin as he wrapped them snugly around her ribs. “But you have it anyway.”

Nesta finally looked at him. Kars knelt beside her, his dark eyes serious, his hands steady. He was younger than most of the warriors here, his face still soft in places, but there was a quiet strength in the way he moved, the way he spoke.

“Why?” The word was sharp, edged with something she couldn’t name.

Kars didn’t look away. “Because you’re the first person here who’s ever made me believe that maybe the rules aren’t as unbreakable as they seem.”

Nesta bared her teeth. “I don’t care about your rules.”

“I know.” A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “But you’re breaking them anyway.”

She wanted to argue, to snap at him, to drive him away with words as sharp as the wind outside. But the truth of it settled over her, heavy and undeniable. She *had* broken them. Not just today, but every day since she’d arrived. The way she refused to bow, the way she fought without honor, the way she looked at them all as if they were beneath her.

And yet, here he was. Kneeling beside her. Offering her something she didn’t want to name.

Kars tied off the bandage and sat back on his heels. “Rest,” he said quietly. “Tomorrow will be worse.”

She didn’t answer. He rose, his movements fluid, and slipped out the door without another word.

The silence that followed was thick, suffocating. Nesta lay back on the pallet, her breath coming in shallow gasps. Outside, the wind howled through the mountains, a mournful sound that echoed the hollow ache in her chest.

She flexed her bruised hands, the pain a reminder of what she’d done. Of what she was capable of.

And what she still had to prove.

---

The barracks roared with laughter, the sound spilling into the night like ale from a cracked mug. Nesta sat on the edge of her pallet, her body a map of aches—each bruise a border, each throb a territory claimed by the day’s training. She should have been asleep. Should have welcomed the oblivion of exhaustion.

But the silence in the hut was worse than the noise outside.

She rose, her legs stiff, and pulled on her boots. The cold bit through the thin soles as she stepped outside, the snow crunching beneath her weight. The moon hung low, a pale coin tossed into the sky, casting long shadows across the training grounds. She walked without thinking, her breath fogging in the air, until the cliffs loomed before her, the drop sheer and merciless.

The wind howled, but it was nothing compared to the storm inside her.

“You should be resting.”

Nesta didn’t turn. She knew that voice—low, rough, like the scrape of a blade against stone. Cassian stepped beside her, his wings folded tight against his back, the crimson of his Siphons dull in the moonlight.

“I’m not tired,” she said.

A lie. She was exhausted. But sleep was just another kind of surrender.

Cassian exhaled, his breath curling between them. “You fought well today.”

The words grated. She didn’t want his praise. Didn’t want his attention. Didn’t want anything from him at all. “Don’t.”

“Don’t what?”

“Don’t act like you care.”

Silence. The wind rushed between them, carrying the distant clink of mugs and the murmur of drunken voices. Cassian shifted, his leather creaking. “I do.”

Nesta’s fingers curled into her palms, her nails biting into the half-healed bruises. She turned to face him, her voice barely above a whisper. “Why?”

His dark eyes held hers, unflinching. “Because I see what you’re becoming.” A pause. “And it terrifies me.”

The words struck like a blow. She wanted to laugh. Wanted to sneer. But her throat was too tight, her chest too full.

She turned away, her heart pounding, and stared out at the mountains. The wind tore at her braid, lashing strands across her face. Cassian didn’t follow. Didn’t speak. His wings rustled once, a whisper of feathers against the dark, and then he was gone.

Back in her hut, Nesta lay on her pallet, her body still humming with the day’s violence. But for the first time since she’d come to these gods-forsaken mountains, she didn’t feel like a prisoner.

She felt like a storm waiting to break.
