CHAPTER 18
The Weight of the Sky

The cold bit into Nesta’s bones before her eyes even opened. She lay still for a moment, letting the ache in her muscles remind her where she was—not in her bed in Velaris, not in the quiet safety of her apartment, but here, in this gods-forsaken camp, where the wind howled like a thing alive and the ground was nothing but rock and ice.

She exhaled, her breath curling in the frigid air, and pushed herself up. The bruises from yesterday’s fight pulsed in time with her heartbeat, a dull, insistent throb. She ignored them, as she ignored the stiffness in her joints, the way her body protested every movement. Ignored, too, the weight of Cassian’s silence—his unspoken question hanging between them like a blade.

The tent was dark, the only light the pale glow of dawn seeping through the flaps. She dressed quickly, her fingers numb as she laced her boots, tied back her hair. The camp outside was eerily quiet. No jeers, no laughter, no taunts from the warriors. Just the whisper of the wind and the distant cry of some bird of prey.

She stepped out into the cold, her breath immediately visible in the air. The warriors were already gathered, their wings folded tight against their backs, their faces unreadable. Cassian stood apart from them, his arms crossed, his Siphons glinting in the dim light. He didn’t look at her.

Devlon stepped forward, his voice cutting through the stillness. “New challenge today.” His eyes flicked to Nesta, cold and assessing. “A race to the highest peak. First to reach the flag wins.”

A murmur rippled through the warriors. Nesta said nothing, her gaze fixed on the jagged outline of the mountain in the distance.

Devlon’s lips curled. “The catch? Nesta will climb. The rest of us fly.”

The warriors laughed, low and cruel. Nesta’s fingers twitched at her sides.

“And we’re under no obligation to play fair,” Devlon added, his grin widening.

Cassian’s jaw tightened, but he didn’t speak.

Nesta didn’t look at him. She didn’t need his pity, his concern. She didn’t need anything from him.

Devlon gestured toward the mountain. “Begin when ready.”

The warriors didn’t hesitate. They launched themselves into the air, their wings beating hard as they soared upward. Nesta watched them go, her breath steady, her body already bracing for the climb.

Bellius lingered for a moment, his wings casting a shadow over her as he hovered just above the ground. He winked. “Good luck, little mortal.”

Then he was gone, joining the others in the sky.

Nesta stood alone at the base of the mountain, the wind tugging at her braid, her breath visible in the cold. She flexed her fingers, rolled her shoulders, and began to climb.

---

The wind howled like a living thing, clawing at Nesta’s braid as she dug her fingers into the jagged rock. The cold bit through her gloves, numbing her fingertips until they felt like blocks of ice. She didn’t dare look down. Not at the sheer drop below, not at Cassian standing at the base with his wings twitching like a caged beast, not at the warriors circling above like vultures scenting blood.

She had known it would be like this. Had known the moment Devlon had sneered at her and said, *Prove you’re worth the air you breathe, girl.* The Illyrians had made their contempt clear from the start—whispers and laughter, the way they’d “accidentally” knocked into her in the mess hall, the way they’d spat at her feet when she passed. But this—this was different. This was war.

A shadow blotted out the sun. Nesta flattened herself against the cliff face just as Bellius dove, a rock clutched in his meaty fist. It grazed her temple before shattering against the stone beside her head. Pain flared, hot and bright, and she hissed as warm blood trickled down her cheek. Above her, Bellius let out a whoop of laughter, wheeling back into the sky to rejoin the others.

*Bastards.*

She wiped the blood away with the back of her hand, her breath coming in sharp, controlled bursts. The wind tried to pry her loose, but she held on, her muscles screaming. She would not fall. She would not give them the satisfaction.

Another rock whistled past her ear. Then another, closer this time, the impact sending a jolt of pain through her knuckles. She gritted her teeth and kept climbing.

Below, Cassian’s wings flared wide, his body tensing as if he might launch himself into the air. But Devlon’s voice cut through the wind like a blade. *“She’s not your concern, warrior.”* The older male’s glare was a physical weight, pinning Cassian in place. His jaw clenched, his Siphons glowing faintly beneath his gloves, but he didn’t move.

Nesta didn’t look at him. She didn’t need his help. Didn’t want it.

A third rock struck her shoulder, hard enough to bruise. She snarled, her fingers curling into invisible claws. The warriors above laughed, their voices carrying on the wind like a taunt.

*Cowards.*

She reached for the next handhold, her fingers slipping on the frost-slicked stone. For a heartbeat, she hung there, her heart hammering against her ribs. Then she found purchase and hauled herself up, her breath ragged.

Kars watched from a distance, his arms crossed over his chest. His expression was unreadable, but his eyes never left her. She wondered if he was waiting for her to fail. Waiting to see if she’d break.

She wouldn’t.

Another shadow loomed. Bellius again, his wings beating the air as he hovered just above her. *“Had enough, human?”* he sneered.

Nesta didn’t answer. She didn’t look up. She just climbed.

A rock struck her thigh, hard enough to make her vision swim. She bit back a cry, her fingers trembling as she reached for the next ledge. The wind howled in her ears, the cold seeping into her bones, but she kept moving. Kept fighting.

Bellius circled once more, his laughter grating. *“Pathetic,”* he spat.

Nesta wiped the blood from her temple, her gaze locking onto him as he hovered above her. She bared her teeth in a silent challenge.

For the first time, Bellius hesitated. His wings faltered, just for a second, before he steadied himself and dove again.

---

The ledge was no wider than her foot, the wind howling up from the abyss below like a living thing. Nesta’s thighs burned, her lungs raw from the thin, icy air. She pressed her back against the rock face, fingers digging into the cold stone as she sucked in a breath. The flag taunted her from above, a scrap of fabric snapping in the gale.

Kars landed beside her with a grunt, his wings folding neatly against his broad back. He offered the waterskin without a word, his dark eyes unreadable. She took it, their fingers brushing for the briefest moment, and drank deeply. The water was warm, almost sweet, but she didn’t thank him. Gratitude was a currency she didn’t deal in.

“They’re not just testing *you*,” Kars said quietly, his voice barely audible over the wind. His gaze flicked upward, toward where Cassian stood with Devlon, their voices raised in argument. “They’re testing *him*.”

Nesta followed his line of sight. Cassian’s wings were half-spread, his crimson Siphons glowing faintly as he gestured toward the cliffs. Devlon’s face was a mask of stubborn fury, his arms crossed over his chest. She couldn’t hear the words, but she didn’t need to. The tension between them was a living thing, coiled and ready to strike.

She handed the waterskin back to Kars, her fingers curling into fists. “What do you mean?”

Before he could answer, the air behind them shifted. A shadow blotted out the sun.

Bellius.

His wings beat once, twice, and then he was upon them, a snarl twisting his lips. He didn’t even bother with a warning. One moment, Kars was standing beside her; the next, he was gone, knocked off the ledge with a brutal shove.

Nesta’s heart lurched into her throat.

Instinct took over. She lunged, her arm snapping out as Kars plummeted. Her fingers closed around his wrist, his weight nearly yanking her off the ledge with him. The rock bit into her ribs, her shoulder screaming in protest as she braced herself. Kars dangled below her, his wings useless in the narrow space, his face pale with shock.

“Hold on,” she gritted out, her muscles trembling with the effort.

Kars’s free hand scrabbled at the rock, but there was nothing to grip. His weight dragged at her, her fingers slipping. She adjusted her hold, her nails digging into his skin. “Pull yourself up,” she hissed.

He obeyed, his muscles straining as he hauled himself upward. The ledge was too narrow for both of them, but she didn’t let go until his chest was pressed against the rock, his breath coming in ragged gasps.

He pulled himself the rest of the way, collapsing onto the ledge beside her. His wings were bent at an awkward angle, one of the primary feathers snapped. He didn’t look at her as he caught his breath, his jaw clenched.

“You saved me,” he said finally, his voice low. “Why?”

Nesta didn’t answer. Her gaze was fixed on Bellius, who had already wheeled away, his laughter ringing out over the cliffs. The flag fluttered above them, mocking.

She could feel the weight of Kars’s stare, but she ignored it. Her body ached, her fingers throbbed where she’d gripped him, but the anger burning in her chest was a far greater pain.

Bellius would pay for that. And so would Devlon.

And Cassian?

She wasn’t sure yet. But she knew one thing: she wasn’t leaving these mountains until she’d made them all regret underestimating her.

---

The wind howled across the jagged peaks, biting through Nesta’s sweat-drenched leathers like a blade. Her lungs burned, her thighs screamed, but she didn’t stop. Not when the rocks beneath her fingers crumbled, not when her boots slipped on the frost-slicked stone. The flag—just a scrap of red fabric tied to a splintered pole—taunted her from the summit, fluttering in the gale like a mocking tongue.

She could hear them below. The jeers, the grunts of effort, the occasional snarl of frustration. The Illyrian warriors had been at this for hours, scaling the cliffs in a race that was as much about endurance as it was about strength. And she, the outsider, the High Fae woman who had never lifted a sword until a few weeks ago, was keeping pace.

Or rather, she *had* been.

Her fingers curled around a jutting rock, her breath coming in ragged gasps. The cold seeped into her bones, but the fire in her chest burned hotter. She wouldn’t let them see her falter. Not again.

A shadow passed overhead, and then Bellius landed on the ledge beside her, his wings flaring wide before folding neatly against his back. His smirk was a knife’s edge, sharp and cruel. The flag was already in his hand, the fabric coiled around his fingers like a trophy.

“Took you long enough,” he drawled, tossing it toward her.

Nesta caught it without thinking, her fingers closing around the rough material. The weight of it was nothing compared to the weight of his gaze, heavy with disdain. She didn’t bother to look at him as she secured the flag to her belt. Let him think it was luck. Let them all think it.

Below, the warriors had stilled. Their murmurs carried on the wind, a low hum of disbelief. She could feel their eyes on her—some curious, some resentful, all of them measuring. She didn’t need to see their faces to know what they were thinking. *She doesn’t belong here.*

Devlon’s voice cut through the silence, gruff and final. “Challenge is over. Bellius wins the race.”

A lie. She had reached the peak first. But the rules had never been fair, not for her.

Bellius stepped closer, his boots scraping against the rock. His breath was warm against her ear when he spoke, low enough that only she could hear. “This isn’t over.”

She didn’t flinch. Didn’t turn. She simply tightened her grip on the flag and waited for him to back away.

When he finally did, she risked a glance downward.

Cassian stood at the base of the cliff, his arms crossed over his chest, his Siphons glinting like drops of blood in the pale morning light. His expression was unreadable, but his eyes—his eyes were fixed on her. For a heartbeat, something flickered in their depths. Not pride. Not approval. Something softer. Something like understanding.

Then he turned away.

The wind howled again, and Nesta stood alone at the peak, the flag clutched in her hand, her gaze locked onto the spot where Cassian had been. The cold bit deeper, but she didn’t move. Not yet.

She wasn’t leaving these mountains until they all regretted underestimating her.

---

The wind howled through the jagged peaks, carrying the scent of snow and pine as Nesta trudged back toward the camp. Her muscles screamed with every step, her bones heavy as if the sky itself had pressed down upon her. The warriors had scattered to their tasks, but she felt their eyes on her—some wide with shock, others narrowed in resentment. She didn’t care. Let them stare. Let them whisper.

She had flown. Not gracefully, not with the effortless power of the Illyrians, but she had *flown*.

And yet, the victory tasted like ash.

The door to her hut loomed ahead, its rough-hewn wood groaning in the wind. She reached for the latch, but a shadow fell over her. Cassian.

His wings were tucked tight against his back, his dark hair tousled from the wind. The crimson Siphons on his knuckles glinted in the fading light, a stark contrast to the leather of his gloves. He didn’t speak at first, just stood there, watching her with an intensity that made her skin prickle.

“You didn’t have to do that,” he said finally, his voice low.

Nesta didn’t turn. “Yes, I did.”

A beat of silence. Then, “Why?”

The question hung between them, heavy as the storm clouds gathering overhead. She could feel the weight of it, pressing down on her chest until her breath came shallow. She wanted to tell him—wanted to say that she refused to be broken, that she would not let them see her cower. But the words stuck in her throat, tangled in the knot of pride and shame and something else she didn’t dare name.

“Because I’m tired of losing,” she whispered.

Cassian exhaled sharply, as if she’d struck him. He stepped closer, the heat of him seeping through the cold that clung to her. “Nesta—”

She whirled on him, her teeth bared. “Don’t.”

His jaw tightened, but he didn’t back away. “You think this is about losing? You think I don’t know what it’s like to feel like you’re drowning?”

Her fingers curled into fists. “I don’t need your pity.”

“It’s not pity,” he growled. “It’s *anger*. At you, for pushing me away. At myself, for not being able to reach you.”

The words struck like a blade, sharp and unrelenting. She wanted to lash out, to drive him away before he could see the cracks in her armor. But before she could speak, his hand lifted, his fingers brushing the sleeve of her tunic.

She jerked back as if burned. “Don’t.”

Cassian froze, his hand hovering in the air between them. His expression shuttered, the raw vulnerability in his eyes snuffed out like a candle. He dropped his arm to his side.

The door slammed between them, the sound echoing through the camp like a gunshot. Nesta stood in the darkness, her chest heaving, her heart pounding so loudly she was sure he could hear it.

But he was already gone.

---

The night air was a blade against her skin, sharp and unrelenting. Nesta stepped outside the rough-hewn barracks, the cold seeping through the thin fabric of her gown as if it were nothing. The Illyrian Mountains loomed around her, their jagged peaks swallowed by the dark, the stars above the only witnesses to her restless pacing.

She had come here to be punished. To be broken, perhaps. But the hollowness inside her remained, a yawning void that even the biting wind couldn’t fill.

Her boots crunched over frost-kissed rocks as she made her way toward the cliffs, the drop beyond a silent, hungry mouth. The camp was quiet now, the warriors either asleep or too exhausted to stir. But she could feel eyes on her—always watching, always waiting for her to crack.

A shadow shifted.

Nesta didn’t turn. She knew the weight of his presence, the way the air hummed when he was near. Cassian stepped beside her, his wings folded tightly against his back, the crimson glow of his Siphons muted in the dark. He didn’t speak. He never did, not when it mattered.

She swallowed. “What if I can’t do this?”

His silence stretched, thick and suffocating. Then, finally, he turned his head, his gaze burning into her. “Then you’ll fail.” His voice was rough, like gravel underfoot. “But you’ll fail knowing you tried.”

Her hands clenched into fists. “And what if I don’t want to try?”

Cassian stepped closer, the heat of him cutting through the cold. “Then you’ll spend the rest of your life wondering what could have been.”

The words struck her like a physical blow. She wanted to lash out, to scream that she didn’t care, that none of it mattered. But the lie tasted bitter on her tongue.

A distant shout echoed through the camp.

They both turned. A group of warriors had gathered around a fire, their voices raised in anger, the flickering light casting long, monstrous shadows. Cassian’s expression darkened, his jaw tightening. “Stay here.”

He didn’t wait for her reply. His wings unfurled, cutting through the darkness as he launched into the night.

Nesta hesitated.

Then she followed.

---

The firelight flickered against the jagged rocks, casting long, wavering shadows over the gathered warriors. Nesta stepped into the clearing, her boots crunching on the frost-laced earth. The air was thick with tension, the kind that coiled in the gut and made the skin prickle.

Bellius stood over Kars, a dagger pressed to the younger warrior’s throat. Kars didn’t move, didn’t breathe, his eyes wide with something between fear and defiance. The other warriors were a storm of noise—some shouting for Bellius to stop, others jeering, their voices a chaotic chorus. Nesta’s fingers curled into fists at her sides.

Then Cassian was there, stepping forward like a blade unsheathed. *"Enough."*

His voice cut through the din, sharp and unyielding. The warriors fell silent, their heads snapping toward him. Bellius didn’t move, his gaze locking onto Nesta. His lip curled. *"This is your fault,"* he snarled. *"You’ve turned them against us."*

Nesta didn’t flinch. She stepped forward, her voice steady, cold. *"No. You did that all by yourself."*

The words hung in the air, heavy as the sky above. The warriors shifted, their gazes darting between Nesta and Bellius, the weight of her words settling over them. Cassian’s wings flared, his Siphons glowing crimson in the firelight. *"Stand down, Bellius."*

Bellius’s grip tightened on the dagger, his knuckles white. His gaze flicked between Nesta and Cassian, the firelight twisting his face into something monstrous. Rage burned in his eyes, hot and unchecked. *"This isn’t over,"* he growled.

Then he was gone, vanishing into the night like smoke on the wind.

The warriors scattered, their murmurs rising like a tide before they melted into the darkness. Only Kars remained, his breath coming in shallow gasps as he pressed a hand to his throat. Nesta didn’t look at him. She didn’t look at Cassian, either.

The fire crackled between them, the only sound in the sudden stillness.

Kars swallowed hard, his voice quiet. *"They’re afraid of you."*
